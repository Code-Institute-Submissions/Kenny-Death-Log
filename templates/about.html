I've never heard of South Park. What is it about?

What!? I assume you've either been in prison for the last three decades or you're trying to be one of the 'edgy' kids of today who are corrupting the moral fabric of society *incoherent grumbling*.....

Nevertheless, you can colour me surprised. I will, however, fill you in. South Park focuses on the lives of the four main characters: Eric Cartman, Stan Marsh, Kyle Broflovski and Kenny McCormick. The boys live in the town of South Park, Colorado. With its snowy peaks and small mountain town life, you wouldn't be mistaken for thinking life in SP would be somewhat uneventful. However, over the past 21 seasons, the residents have encountered aliens, holy prophets, radical ginger kids, hordes of Peruvian pan-flute players, sociopathic therapists suffering form Multiple Personality Disorder, a mecha Barbara Streisand and the Mongols.

The older series focused on fictitious storylines created by Matt Stone and Trey Parker. However, the later seasons have used real life scenarios and political sagas to shape their episodes. This has led to some humorous situations, such as the recent addition of the PC Principal character as well as long standing favourite, school teacher Mr. Garrison, becoming President Trump, complete with festive orange glow. Certainly not for the faint of heart, this cartoon definitely isn't one for the kids. South Park's reputation for deliberately causing offence has led to its dominance as one of the longest running animated series of all time. While being purposefully provocative, it has always escaped most negative attention, as viewers consider it a tongue-in-cheek breath of fresh air, especially in the current mad politically correct climate we live in.
