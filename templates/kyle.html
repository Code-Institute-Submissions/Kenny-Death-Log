Kyle was always going to have a hard life in South Park being a Jew. Not that the town is in any way anti-semitic, but seeing as Cartman once dressed as Hitler and led a march through town against the Jews (the people had no idea what the German chants meant), little more needs to be said. Kyle and Cartman's rivalry has provided some of the funniest moments of the show's history, with Cartman blaming every one of Kyle's shortcomings on his religion.

Perhaps the only other voice of reason in South Park bar Stan, it's no surprise that the two of them are the best of friends. Once the shenanigans are over, it's usually Kyle's usual line of, "You know, I learned something today..." that closes the show. Somewhat cautious (which is naturally due to his religion if you listen to Cartman), Kyle often proves to be the wet blanket who tries to do the right thing at all times. This can make him somewhat irritating at times, but nevertheless still an integral part of the show.
